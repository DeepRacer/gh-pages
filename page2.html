The evolution of city planning - intelligent cities.

Why not use the term 'smart cities' ?  Because we hate buzz words... although true - not really. We switched only temporarily in fact, so that we will reserve that as the end goal when we finally have general A.I. to help manage the entiriety of our hyper-complex cities - still many decades out.  For now, we care to make intelliegent components, and specific stakeholders 'intelligent' - simply enough, the goal is to give back time to the city inhabitant. Giving back time, is what we consider as the hallmark success metric of any good robotics innovation.

Proper City Planning is the foundation of a collaborative and organized culture.  City planning should shift from being centered around productivty, but shouldn't forget to factor in the general well being of city inhabitants, as well.

Exponential population growth and unfettered city expansion has led to the fragmentation of our city and mega cities. Historically we have been designing cities around the personal vehicle, and the tools for simulation and for grasping such an enornmous prediciton, was simply not part of city planning discussions.

At 3DCityIO we strongly believe that accelerating Robotics & A.I. via readily accesible HD maps, will help us start solving these cumulative problems, and give us that necessary 'pulse' and 'city vitals' to measure success in design i.e. happiness of the citizen.  We are starting like most, with mobility - but with a twist.

3DCityIO will readily produce on-demand 3D maps with custom overlays,  Maps-as-a-Service that is accesible to anyone, at a reasonable cost. We aim to provide a central repository of high-definition 3D/4D/XD mapping data so that large simulations can collaborate.  All is easily accessed e.g. for use in simulations, SLAM, IoT innovation and more.  The idea being that in general, contextual data overlays and ground truth are essential to progress - hence, should be shared..

Bringing Back Human Centric Design : Intelligent cities ( Narrow A. I. ) -> Conscious cities ( City A. I. )

 The next layer is to model our cities towards a more human centric design. Driving 2 hours each way to work, is inane - now what do we do about it? There are certain metabolic pathways in the cell, much like what is seen in a more macro-scale 'living' - 'breathing' city. If one eliiminates such a pathway, the cell much like the city, ceases to exist. These are what are what systems biologiest considered 'prohibitive deletions' - these need to be identified, and nurtured, all other non-essential transport needs to find paths of 'less resistance' i.e. increase adaptability, increase the level of real-time homeostasis

Borrowed from Systems Biology principles we believe our cities behave more like a living cell.  Humanity has looked to nature to get hints to solve engineering problems,  (e.g. neural nets, metabolic systems).  A city has usually risen by an industrial impetus, shaping the flavor and essence of the town before becoming np-HARD.  Once the industry subsides, so often does the city.  We need to make our cities more resilient, nimble, and adaptable to our rapidly changing needs and environment - something difficult to do in face of industrial/population pressures.
